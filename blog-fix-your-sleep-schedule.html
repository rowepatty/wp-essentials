5 Items That Will Fix Your Sleep Schedule (from a former cadet)

Patrick Rowe | 7/21/2026

My experience

Two years ago, when I had finished a grueling six weeks of Beast, I was missing my bed back home. The standard issue green girl and pillow didn’t quite measure up to what I left behind.

As a cadet candidate, I hadn’t even thought about life after Beast. The Academic Year proved to be a battle of its own, and I was ill-prepared. The AY is full of many different challenges, but what’s the biggest challenge for every cadet?

Sleep!

Why is it so difficult to sleep?

First, there is always work to do. West Point takes up your day with briefs, taskings, formations, mandatory meals, and class, just to name a few. You attend to your duties, and your homework piles up. Soon enough, your bed becomes your refuge against a demanding cadet life.

Second, there is no comfort. The barracks are unbelievably warm. It is very likely that the A/C will break and will not be repaired. It is also very likely that your chain of command will order everyone to keep their windows shut. Without comfortable bedding, you will be tossing and turning all night. I certainly was.

Getting Lost on Amazon

When I started shopping for bedding items on Amazon, I was completely lost. I had never shopped for my own pillow or blankets. It is especially difficult to balance comfort, maintainability, and the cadet room regulations.

My guide for you

To guide anyone who is feeling lost, I created a list of bedding items that I have personally recommended to my friends. I have used 4 of these items personally while at West Point, and last is recommended by multiple past roommates. All these items are comfortable, easy to wash, keep you cool, and are within the regulations.

2. Weighted cooling blanket (better than melatonin)

A cooling blanket is an absolute must have. The barracks are unbelievably warm in the summer, especially after your chain of command tells you that your windows must be closed.

This is the only cooling blanket I have ever owned, and it has the additional benefit of being weighted. The weight calms you down before you go to sleep, so the stress of West Point can’t get you in bed.

I originally bought it because it ticks all the boxes: machine washable, highly rated, moderately priced, and cooling properties. I stuck with it because it is so effective. I am still sleeping with it today!

3. Cooling memory foam pillow (support and comfort)

This is the best pillow I have ever owned, and after my mom got me one, my entire family followed soon after. It stays cold all night, rests your head at the perfect height, and easily rolls up to bring home on leave.

The pillow’s size can be adjusted by removing memory foam. For me, its original size is perfect for West Point: it props up your head just enough to comfortably read a book or look at your phone while in bed, since you don’t have a headboard anymore.

And I can personally attest that I don’t flip my pillow to the cold side anymore!

4. The manta sleep mask (protects against night-owl roommates)

This is by far the best sleep mask I have ever used, and one of the most popular among cadets at West Point. It fits snug (but not too snug) and is comfortable on the face. It completely blocks out light, unlike flat sleep masks. And it stays cold.

Before I used this sleep mask, I bought two others for around $20 each. Neither of them lasted long; they didn’t block light well, were uncomfortable on the face, and made my head hot.

The Manta sleep mask is a guarantee. If you buy the Manta, you will never buy another sleep mask.

5. Desk fan (necessary for survival)

There is an extremely high chance that the barracks A/C will break. You will be unbearably warm. Sweating outside between classes is bad, but sweating in your bed is miserable.

You don’t need a special fan to do the job. Sometimes the best answer is the simplest. And in this case, the Amazon-brand fan is all you need. It blows cold air and it won’t break, no matter how much you use it.

Further Improvements

There are other items that can improve your sleep that I have never used. I can’t personally recommend them, so I will mention them here:

  • If your roommates are loud at night, you need ear plugs or AirPod Pros. Either one is sufficient, but one is obviously cheaper.
  • Many people like to use an air purifier or humidifier. It can help remove any residual smell in your room.
  • If you read before you sleep, there are small reading lamps that clip to your book.
  • If you have trouble waking up to your iPhone alarm, consider a shock bracelet. DO NOT get a normal alarm; you will wake up the entire room.

Closing

Thank you for reading! I hope this guide helped you, and I hope you sleep comfortably.
